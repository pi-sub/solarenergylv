Windows

In Las Vegas, keeping the hot air out and the cool air in -- and vice versa -- is always a challenge. Double pane windows offer homeowners greater energy efficiency than single pane windows. Double pane windows contain two panes of glass separated by a space of gas or air that serves as a buffer and helps to slow the transfer between hot and cold air. To learn more about this home improvement incentive, speak with one of our energy consultants or schedule a free, no obligation home evaluation today.

Roof

A properly installed new roof can provide a strong seal against the elements and lower both your air conditioning and heating costs. In addition, dark color roofs tend to attract and maintain heat which can cause your A/C to work harder and result in higher energy bills, especially during the summer months when temperature reach triple digits for days and weeks at a time. By selecting a lighter colored roof, you can drastically reduce your energy bill. To learn more about this home improvement incentive, speak with a Solar Energy Las Vegas consultant today or schedule a free, no obligation home evaluation.

Insulation

According to the North American Insulation Manufacturers Association, 90% of single-family homes in the U.S. are under-insulated, so chances are high that your house could use some work. The EPA estimates that the average homeowner can save 15% on heating and cooling costs (11% of total energy costs) by adding insulation in attics and crawl spaces. A well-insulated home often commands a higher market value. Recent studies estimate that there’s at least a break-even return on investment regarding your home’s value after installing insulation.
